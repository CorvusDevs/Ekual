Why Ekual

Why you need loudness equalization

Every app, website, and media file is mastered at a different volume level. Your Mac doesn't fix this — but Ekual does.

The volume rollercoaster

You're watching a quiet dialogue scene, then an ad blasts at full volume. You switch from a podcast to a YouTube video and scramble for the volume keys. Different apps, different websites, different streaming services — they all output audio at wildly different levels. Without equalization, you're constantly adjusting volume by hand.

How Ekual solves it

Ekual applies real-time dynamic range compression to your Mac's system audio. It monitors the loudness of the audio stream, brings quiet sounds up and loud sounds down to meet a consistent target level. The result: everything plays at a comfortable, even volume — whether it's a whisper-quiet video call or a booming action movie trailer.

Windows has it. macOS doesn't.

Windows has included a "Loudness Equalization" option in its audio settings for over a decade. macOS has never offered this feature. Ekual fills that gap — giving Mac users the same volume consistency that Windows users have had for years, with a cleaner implementation and more control.

Requires macOS 14.2 or later.
